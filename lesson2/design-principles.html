Rule of Thirds

Nathan Pacheco

www.nathanpacheco.com/

The rule of thirds is a simple but powerful design idea where one will divide a page or canvas into thirds in order to create something that is more visually appealing that placing everything perfectly centered or to the side. It allows for a simple, yet attracted model to help the viewer begin looking straight ahead but not look to far to find what they're looking for. In other words, it is a proven way to make something more appealing and maneagable. Artists do this by dividing a page into thirds horizontally and/vertically to be used for design.

Hick's Law

Death Cab for Cutie

www.deathcabforcutie.com/

Hick's law means that the more choices available to an individual, the longer it takes for them to decide what to pick. For this reason, it's important in designing to place emphasis on one or only a few important things you are trying to convey or bring people to. Too many links in a website, for example, might leave a person lingering and eventually giving up on the website altogether without ever finding the main reason you'd like them to be there in the first place.

PARC: Proximity

Taylor Swift

www.taylorswift.com/

Like the name implies, proximity refers to how close objects are together. In the case of this Taylor Swift example, I found it visually appealing in the sense that it uses different sizes of the same profile picture but in different locations causing one to feel a sense of proximity to the entire page as one reads through the content. I think most artist pages avoid a lot of writing, but this grabs one in and makes the proximity of these photos, being the same photo, seem connected and allows the readers to feel as if they are inside the photo itself.
